Design thinking process

As part of designing and developing this portfolio webiste for myself, I needed to have a basic understanding of design. The thinking process used in designing this website is taken from NNGroup.com and I will be using this framework from this point on.

Empathise stage

The first stage requires me to uncover and find out what users to my website would want to see in my portfolio. My website would have to fulfill their needs, or else users would simply scroll through without reading or worse, they would exit my website altogether. To understand the needs of users, I have to decided to conduct a simple survey to find out what users want to see when they enter my website. The target audience of the survey would reflect the demographics of the future users to my site.

The objective of this survey is to identify any potential painpoints or unnecessary features in my website.

Survey results

The survey distributed has garnered 32 responses, many of whom belong to the targeted demographics of the survey.

Showcasing skill sets

The results shows that 56.3% agreed that listing down skill-sets is very important in an online portfolio.

Having a brief introduction

An overwhelming majoring of 94% agreed that having a brief description of oneself is very important in an online portfolio.

Featuring past projects

From the survey, there has been varying opinions on the importance of including past projects to the online portfolio. However, the majority still agrees that it is somewhat important.

Having testimonials

From the survey, the majority of respondents or 34% are neutral on whether having testimonials would help in an online portfolio. However, it can be seen that the other respondents agrees that it is important.

Providing social handles

The majority of respondents agrees that providing socials in an online portfolio is important.

Define stage

This stage requires a problem statement to be properly defined. From the results of the survey, I now have a sense of what potential users expect from my portfolio. One method that designers use to define a problem statement would be to create an empathy map. An empathy map would illustrate a user, through a made-up persona. The following are empathy maps for Alan; a potential employer and Samantha: a future colleague.

Empathy Maps

From these personas, I am able to come up with a problem statement, which is necessary in the ideating stage in a later stage.

Problem statement: Users to my portfolio website would need a way to get details on my work abilities, skills and experience for the job I applied for because they would not want to hire someone that is not suitable for the job.

Ideate stage

It is during this stage that I am able to explore the features that would be added into the website. From the results gathered from the user research survey, we have determined that users want to see my work skills, abilities and experience. This is where I would be finding potential solutions this. To accomplish this, a flow sketch could be utillised. A flow sketch wireframe would ensure that the solutions that I would come up with is user-focused, defines the website features and is quick to create.

Low fidelity flow-sketch

With help from other coursemates who are also working on their own websites, we collaborated and drew several flow sketches. After a dozen of sketches, I settled on the sketch above for the low fidelity flow-sketch.

Prototype stage

At this stage, I have a clear idea of what is needed to be included in the portfolio website. From the problem statement and survey results gathered, I have decided that the following must be included:
1) A section where I showcase my skills and past works.
2) A testimonials section from past and current colleagues and bosses.
3) A contact me section where people may contact me with.

Testing stage

This stage would commence after the entire website has been made. To gain insightful feedback from users, a qualitative usability testing has to be carried out. This would uncover any problems that users would face.

The usability testing plan is as follows:
Goal: To determine the effectiveness of the portfolio website.
Scope To test how engaging the website is to users by looking if the users visits every page in the website.
Profile of participants 25-45, tech-savy
Number of participants Qualitative, 6 pax
Setting of Utility testing In-person, unmoderated
User goals: Navigate through the site and find a service that you are most likely to engage in and to engage the person for services.

Usability testing results

The test was conducted in-person to 6 people. During the tests, participants were left on their own, and only given the two tasks to complete. It was only after their tasks were completed where they were asked on the difficulties they faced.

Tasks 1 Find out what the portfolio website offers.
Tasks 2 You are certain that you want to engage the person for a service. What would you do next?

Below are the results to the tasks:

As seen, both tasks are successfully completed by all 6 participants. This means there are no major problems to the site. However it is noted that there are some feedback which would covered in the next section.
